One place for the details that follow your family around.

HolyFHIR gives you a structured home for information that tends to scatter across portals, PDFs, pharmacy bags, discharge papers, school forms, care notes, and memory.

P

Family profiles

Keep a profile for each person you care for with conditions, allergies, medications, visits, immunizations, and documents nearby.

F

Portal records

Bring in supported FHIR JSON, NDJSON, bundles, and requested-record ZIP exports while keeping the original source snapshots.

D

Appointment papers

Organize PDFs, reports, visit summaries, labs, school notes, care instructions, and other records so they are easier to find when appointments come up.

T

Growth and trends

Chart numeric observations such as weight, blood pressure, glucose, vitals, and lab values over time.

Built for the night-before-the-appointment scramble.

Open source, so trust can be earned in the open.

HolyFHIR is open source, which means the code can be inspected instead of treated like a black box. For a family health-record tool, that matters: people can see how the app stores data, handles imports, and makes tradeoffs.

Open source does not make software automatically safe or correct, but it does make the project more accountable. Bugs, privacy concerns, and design choices can be found, discussed, and improved in public.
